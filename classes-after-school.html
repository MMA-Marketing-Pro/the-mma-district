Why the After School Program

The hours after school shape a kid's habits more than almost anything else. Our after-school program turns that time into real growth — a safe, structured environment where kids move, learn, and build themselves up. Every session blends martial arts skill with fitness, self-defense, and character: they burn energy, learn to handle themselves, and walk out more confident than they walked in. No experience needed, no ego on the mats — just great coaching and a lot of hard work that feels like fun.

What They'll Build
  1. Real martial arts skill: Kickboxing striking and Jiu-Jitsu control, taught safely
  2. Practical self-defense: awareness, boundary-setting, and escaping grabs
  3. Fitness & coordination: agility, core strength, and conditioning through play
  4. Confidence & discipline: focus, effort, and respect for training partners
  5. Anti-bullying tools: calm confidence and the skills to back it up
The Curriculum · Six Tracks

A complete, age-appropriate curriculum.

Coaches pull from six tracks and scale them to each group's age and level — from the safe fundamentals of movement to real-world self-defense for older kids.

Track 1 · Comprehensive Physical Education

The safe foundation. Core fitness, coordination, agility, and non-contact martial arts movement — functional fitness (animal movements, calisthenics, core strength) plus basic stance, footwork, and balance.

Track 2 · Modern Self-Defense & Empowerment

De-escalation, situational awareness, and boundary-setting. Verbal skills for handling bullies, confidence-building posture, escaping grabs (wrist releases, body locks), and managing distance and spatial awareness.

Track 3 · Ground Defense & Jiu-Jitsu

Leverage, control, and defense from the ground without striking. Positional dominance (guard, side control, mount), sweeps, and escapes — with the emphasis on control over submissions for younger students.

Track 4 · Kickboxing Fitness & Mechanics

Cardiovascular endurance, rhythm, and striking mechanics on pads and shields. Proper punching and kicking technique, focus-mitt and kick-shield drills (no sparring), and high-intensity martial arts circuits.

Track 5 · Tactical Fitness & Athlete Conditioning

Strength and conditioning built for young athletes: circuit training, agility ladders, core stability, and plyometrics — power, endurance, and durability that carry into every sport.

Track 6 · Real-World Threat Management

For older kids and teens navigating real pressures — bullying, peer pressure, and intimidation. Situational awareness, de-escalation, escaping grabs, handling a larger aggressor, and the psychology of body language and confidence.
